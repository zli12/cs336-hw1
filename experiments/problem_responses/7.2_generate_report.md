## Fluency Commentary

These outputs are quite fluent for a 4-layer / `d_model=512` / 22 M-parameter model trained on ~327 M TinyStories tokens. They reproduce the dataset's distinctive style well: short declarative sentences, named protagonists ("Tim", "Sue", "Mia", "Lily"), simple turn-taking dialogue with quoted speech, and a moral / wrap-up at the end. Grammar is consistently clean (subject-verb agreement, tense consistency, pronoun anaphora), entities are tracked across multi-paragraph stories, and the model terminates each story naturally with `<|endoftext|>`, indicating it has learned document boundaries.

The new (cosine, val 1.349) checkpoint produces a clear improvement over the previous (fixed-LR, val 1.475) checkpoint: dialogue is more natural ("I like your red bucket too, Tim"), entity tracking is tighter (Tim and Sue stay consistent over the full story), and there are fewer instances of the "stuck on a phrase" pattern we saw earlier (e.g. the older model's "play the game" repeated 5+ times in one story, or its butterfly-becomes-a-tree-protector chain). At ~10% perplexity reduction we get a visible jump in coherence, although typical small-LM weaknesses still show up locally - e.g. the Mia story has a small drift where the mom tells Mia to put her toys *away*, but Mia is described as "happy to help her mom" instead of acknowledging the rebuke; and the Tim/Ben sample has the surreal "fake gun" subplot the model didn't quite know how to land. These are characteristic of small-model TinyStories generations: plausible-but-shallow narrative arcs.

## Factors That Affect Output Quality

Two factors I observed directly, plus a few additional ones worth noting:

1. **Training compute / total tokens seen.** Both checkpoints saw the same ~327M tokens, but the LR schedule changes how efficiently those tokens are converted into loss reduction. The 10K cosine schedule reaches val 1.3489, while the same compute under fixed LR reaches only 1.4536 - a 0.10-nat / 10%-perplexity gap that translates directly into more coherent generations. The reference example in the assignment looks similarly fluent to our cosine output. The "low-resource" 40 M-token sample shown in the handout is visibly shakier, consistent with this.
2. **Decoding hyperparameters (`temperature`, `top_p`).** Lower temperature (`0.7`) gave more deterministic, "safer" stories with higher repetition of common patterns. Higher temperature (`1.0`) introduced more variety but also more odd subplots (the "fake gun" detour above). `temperature=0.8, top_p=0.95` was the best balance: enough randomness for variety, enough truncation to keep token choices on-distribution. With no nucleus truncation (`top_p=1.0`) or very high temperature, the model drifts into ungrammatical low-probability tails.
3. **LR schedule (warmup + cosine decay).** Beyond just *total* tokens, the *shape* of LR over training matters. The cosine decay's late-stage low-LR phase lets the model fine-tune to a sharper minimum, which is what closed the 0.10-nat gap. Without warmup the model also benefits from less early-step instability.
4. **Tokenizer / vocab quality.** A 10k BPE vocab trained on TinyStories captures common words and morphology cleanly, so the model rarely splits a real word into bizarre subwords. A poorly trained or mismatched tokenizer would break fluency immediately.
5. **Model capacity (depth, width, heads).** Our model is small (4 layers, `d_model=512`, ~22M params). A deeper / wider model at the same training budget tends to track entities and longer-range dependencies more accurately, reducing the kind of mid-story drift we saw in the Mia / "fake gun" subplots.
6. **Architecture choices (RMSNorm, RoPE, SwiGLU).** Our 7.3 ablations confirm these choices each contribute meaningfully - removing any of them at the same training budget yields a measurably worse validation loss, which directly shows up as lower fluency at decode time.

## Reproduction Commands

```bash
uv run python scripts/generate_lm.py \
  --checkpoint-path experiments/checkpoints/tinystories-lr-tuned-cosine.pt \
  --vocab-path experiments/tinystories-bpe-10k-vocab.json \
  --merges-path experiments/tinystories-bpe-10k-merges.txt \
  --prompt "Once upon a time" \
  --max-new-tokens 320 --temperature 0.8 --top-p 0.95 \
  --eos-token "<|never|>" \
  --vocab-size 10000 --context-length 256 \
  --d-model 512 --num-layers 4 --num-heads 16 --d-ff 1344 --rope-theta 10000 \
  --device cuda
```

(Set `--eos-token "<|endoftext|>"` to stop at the first end-of-text token.)
